Finding Worry

Finding Worry is an easy step-by-step card game aimed to create a relaxing atmosphere of conversation abuot low mood. The game includes three types of images ,nature, everyday objects and abstract shapes, and one topic dice which is aimed to be a trigger for people to think of.

Make Your Finding Worry

Instruction
Step 1

Choose the role. One Listener, One Teller

arrow_downward
Step 2

Teller pickes four cards and use them to describe one daily worry.

arrow_downward
Step 3

Listener pickes four cards and use them to give a tip to Listener.

Elements
  • Topic Dice

    Use a cube-shaped object, an eraser or paper folded. Then, write down six topics on each side.

  • Abstract Shape x 8

    Draw the simple shapes on papers. They can be circle, square, or shapes combined with simple shapes.

  • Everyday Object x 8

    As this is Everyday Object, you can use the objects already around you or find their images from magazines, leaflets or flyers.

  • Nature x 10

    Find out magazines, leaflets or flyers with nature pictures on it and cut down them.

About

The project is aimed to intervent the accumlated low mood and encourage people to talk about the negative emotions.

Talking plays a vital role for asking for help. However, it is not easy for some people. Thus, we amplified the good part of talking therapy and implanted it to the daily life. The outcome is a card game aimed to create a relaxing atmosphere and visualise invisible emotions.

Research Book

In the whole research process, we did the research about various therapies and environmental study. Further, we interviewed the people who have experienced the talking therapy for a long time and a talking therapist in London. After prototyping the card game, we experimented the game in different places from someone’s home to mental clinic with different users from children to adults.
